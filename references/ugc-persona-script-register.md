# UGC And Persona Script Register

Use this reference for short-video voice-over, UGC/KOC/KOS scripts, vlogs, character monologues, TTS scripts, or large persona/script banks.

## Core Judgment

The problem is rarely “too much first person.” The high-risk pattern is **conclusion-heavy first person**: every speaker uses “我” to explain the meaning of the scene, state a complete value judgment, and close with a lesson or engagement question. The labels change, but the hidden copywriter remains audible.

Keep first person when it performs one of these jobs:

- **Action**: what the speaker did, changed, checked, forgot, or refused.
- **Perception**: what the speaker noticed through a concrete detail.
- **Reaction**: what the speaker said or did in the moment.
- **Uncertainty**: what the speaker still cannot decide or explain.
- **Limited judgment**: a conclusion bounded by this event, not a universal lesson.

Treat these as high risk:

- “我终于明白 / 我才意识到 / 对我来说 / 最重要的是……”
- “真正的 X 是…… / 这才是…… / 这就是……”
- “比起 A，B 更重要 / 更有意义 / 更有分量。”
- a final question added only to request comments
- a polished life lesson no real speaker would formulate while the event is happening

## Build A Speaker Before Writing Lines

For each recurring persona, define a small language system. Identity and values are not enough.

| Dimension | Useful definition |
| --- | --- |
| Immediate objective | What the person is trying to finish, avoid, choose, hide, or protect in this scene |
| Attention pattern | What they notice first: time, cost, safety, other people's expressions, product behavior, status, or convenience |
| Knowledge limit | What they know from experience and what they cannot confidently explain |
| Sentence shape | Short fragments, detailed chronology, self-interruption, dry observation, technical comparison, or emotional spill |
| Vocabulary | Words this person naturally uses, including occupation/family-specific language |
| Emotional temperature | Calm, impatient, embarrassed, excited, defensive, tired, restrained |
| Avoided language | Phrases this person would never say, especially polished summaries and brand wording |

### Persona-Blind Test

Remove the persona labels from two scripts. If the lines can be swapped without feeling wrong, the persona work has failed. Change what each speaker notices, knows, omits, and how they form sentences; do not merely add slang or catchphrases.

## Script Construction

### Start From An Event, Not A Metric Formula

Do not begin every script with “3-second hook -> conflict -> feature -> insight -> comment question.” Use the platform goal as a constraint, not visible narration. Vary the entry point:

- an interrupted action
- a direct quote
- an object already in the speaker's hand
- a mistake or failed attempt
- a decision still being made
- a visible contradiction
- a late realization expressed through behavior, not a thesis

### Separate Image And Voice

For every line, ask what the viewer can already see.

- **Image**: visible action, object, location, product behavior, facial response.
- **Voice**: motive, missing context, private doubt, off-camera consequence, remembered quote.

If voice and image say the same thing, delete or redirect the voice. “I opened the trunk” adds nothing when the shot shows the trunk opening. “I had already repacked it twice” adds unseen context.

### Let The Ending Stay In The Scene

Prefer one of these endings:

- a concrete next action
- somebody else's short quote
- a physical reaction
- an unresolved choice
- a small contradiction
- a visual callback to the opening
- a specific question that naturally follows the scene

Do not force elevation. If deleting the last sentence makes the script stronger and nothing factual is lost, delete it.

## Rewrite Procedure For A Script Bank

1. Inventory every script and duplicate surface: title, persona, voice direction, full script, shot table, caption, and ending.
2. Group recurring personas and write one language-system card per group.
3. Mark each first-person sentence as action, perception, reaction, uncertainty, limited judgment, or conclusion.
4. Keep or sharpen the first five types. Delete or rewrite unsupported conclusions.
5. Compare image and voice line by line; remove direct duplication.
6. Run ending deletion tests. Keep questions only when specific and earned.
7. Run the persona-blind test across adjacent and distant scripts.
8. Search the full bank for the rejected pattern, not just the sample the user flagged.
9. Recheck duplicate full-script and shot-table copies after editing.
10. Compare every added scene detail against the source. Reject any new quote, action, object, location, number, reaction, or relationship detail without explicit support.

## Batch Gates

Do not claim a large script bank is fully revised until all gates pass:

- **Coverage gate**: every script and every duplicate surface has been reviewed or explicitly excluded.
- **Conclusion gate**: no repeated “我终于明白 / 最重要 / 真正 / 这才是” ending formula remains without scene-specific justification.
- **Persona gate**: recurring personas differ in attention, knowledge, syntax, and emotional temperature.
- **Voice/image gate**: voice adds information the image cannot supply.
- **Ending gate**: takeaways and questions are not mandatory template slots.
- **TTS gate**: the text can be spoken naturally; punctuation does not hide written-language syntax.
- **Fact gate**: no invented experiences, reactions, dialogue, or product results were added.
- **Provenance gate**: every newly added concrete noun, action, quote, number, and observed reaction can be pointed back to the original script, shot description, persona card, or approved fact sheet. "Plausible" is not evidence.

## Minimal Revision Rule

When source detail is limited, deletion is safer than fabrication. Remove the generic lesson and let the concrete scene stand. Do not manufacture dialect, typos, family conflict, or vulnerability merely to simulate authenticity.

Do not convert an unsupported lesson into an invented cinematic beat. If the source only says “这次安排就对了,” a rewrite may delete that sentence or keep the preceding verified action. It may not add “他笑着拍了拍我” or a new direct quote unless the source already contains it.
