# Executive And Research Report Register

Use this for executive summaries, decision briefs, management updates, research conclusions, performance reviews, analytical reports, and data-led recommendations.

## Reader And Function

The reader is deciding what to believe, what matters now, what to do next, and what risk remains. Respect limited attention without removing the evidence needed to make the decision.

## Information Order

1. **Decision or finding**: state the main judgment early.
2. **Evidence**: show the few facts or comparisons that support it.
3. **Implication**: explain what changes for the business, plan, or resource decision.
4. **Recommendation**: name the owner, action, timing, or decision required.
5. **Risk and uncertainty**: state assumptions, missing data, and failure conditions.

## Register Rules

- Use concise professional Chinese with a high ratio of judgment to explanation.
- Separate fact, interpretation, forecast, and recommendation.
- Use headings that state the finding or decision, not generic categories such as "背景分析" or "价值意义."
- Keep numbers comparable: period, denominator, scope, and baseline must not drift.
- Do not replace uncertainty with confident wording.
- Do not turn the report into an SOP. Operational detail belongs in an appendix or follow-up section unless it changes the decision.

## Research-Specific Checks

- Correlation is not automatically causation.
- A sample observation is not automatically a market conclusion.
- A forecast must state its assumptions.
- A percentage without denominator or comparison period is incomplete.
- "显著、领先、提升" requires a defined comparison.

## Executive-Specific Checks

- The opening should answer what changed and why the reader should care.
- Recommendations must state what decision or resource is needed.
- Risks should name impact and trigger, not merely say "存在不确定性."
- Repeated background should be cut before evidence or decisions are cut.

## Final Check

- Can the reader identify the main decision in the first screen or page?
- Is each recommendation supported by evidence at the available level?
- Are assumptions and unknowns visible?
- Does the text sound like a decision document rather than a slogan, transcript, or project diary?
