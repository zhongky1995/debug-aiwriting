# AI Trace Checklist

Use this checklist to diagnose Chinese text that feels generated, over-polished, or generic. Do not apply every item mechanically; judge against the genre and reader.

## Structural Traces

- Opens with a broad definition, era claim, or universal statement before saying anything concrete.
- Uses a predictable "提出问题 -> 分析原因 -> 给出建议 -> 总结升华" structure for every topic.
- Relies on "首先、其次、最后、综上所述、总的来说、值得注意的是" as visible scaffolding.
- Headings are too symmetrical: "不只是 X，更是 Y", "从 A 到 B", "重新定义 X".
- Every section ends with a mini-summary or slogan-like takeaway.
- The ending merely repeats the thesis with grander words.

## Language Traces

- Abstract nouns pile up: 价值、赋能、生态、闭环、抓手、路径、底层逻辑、范式、体系、维度、颗粒度.
- Professional filler replaces action: 承接、整合、节点、触点、阵地、资产、沉淀、方法论、可复用、可迁移、经营、链路 appear without actor, action, object, timing, or result.
- Abstract verbs are paired with abstract nouns: 赋能增长、激活心智、撬动势能、释放价值、构建闭环、完成转化前置.
- Case/report language uses labels such as 用户承接、内容整合、复购节点、服务角色、关系经营, but never says what actually happens.
- Jargon is replaced by softer jargon: 重新接回服务链路、进入沟通节奏、沉淀回访名单、打通复购触点, instead of plain actions a person would actually say.
- The verb itself is unnatural: 接回、接住、打通、沉淀、激活、撬动、承接、触达 are paired with objects they would not naturally take in spoken or clear written Chinese.
- Results are written as actions: 实现心智占位、完成用户教育、形成转化闭环, without saying what a team or user actually does.
- The subject is missing: the sentence says "实现、完成、形成、释放", but no person, team, user, page, channel, or material performs the action.
- Sentences are correct but consequence-free: they sound plausible without naming who does what, when, where, or under what constraint.
- Phrases hedge too much: 在一定程度上、可以说、某种意义上、相对而言、不可忽视.
- Phrases overclaim too much: 无疑、显著、深刻、全面、极大、革命性、前所未有.
- Uses repeated contrast templates: 不是...而是..., 不仅...更..., 既...又..., 一方面...另一方面....
- Overuses punctuation as structure: colon explanations, quotation marks around ordinary terms, repeated em dashes or semicolon-like pauses.

## Evidence Traces

- Gives recommendations without tradeoffs, prerequisites, or failure cases.
- Mentions "用户、品牌、企业、团队、市场" without narrowing the actor.
- Uses examples that are categories rather than examples: "比如在营销、运营、管理等场景".
- Turns weak material into confident conclusions.
- Adds false balance when one side is clearly more relevant.

## Proposal / Sales Material Traces

- Starts from "我们能做什么" instead of the client's decision question.
- Lists services, channels, or modules without explaining the mechanism that connects them.
- Says "全域、整合、闭环、生态、矩阵、赋能" but does not name the handoff, owner, timing, metric, or failure condition.
- Treats all users, channels, or resources as one group instead of segmenting by stage, role, intent, or resource position.
- Uses a case as decoration, but does not say what the case proves for this client's problem.
- Promises outcomes without saying which assumptions, inputs, or execution conditions are required.
- Writes execution confidence without a minimum proof object: role action, timeline, SOP, script, checklist, data field, report, or review rhythm.
- Ends with "期待合作 / 共创增长" instead of a concrete next decision, scope boundary, or diagnostic step.

## Rhythm Traces

- Paragraphs have similar length and all move at the same speed.
- Many sentences end with "的过程 / 的能力 / 的方式 / 的重要性".
- Every paragraph begins with a connective phrase.
- The draft is too smooth: no emphasis, no friction, no local judgment.
- Bullets are parallel but interchangeable.

## Chinese-Specific Traces

- Translationese: "在...方面进行提升", "对于...而言", "通过...来实现".
- Nominalization where a verb would be clearer: "进行优化" instead of "优化"; "实现转化" instead of "转化".
- Formality mismatch: small practical topics are written like white papers; serious reports are forced into internet slang.
- "你会发现 / 我们不难发现 / 这背后其实是" appears as a generic reveal device.

## Severity Guide

- **Light**: a few template transitions or stiff phrases. Line edit is enough.
- **Medium**: structure is generic but content is usable. Rebuild section order and rewrite openings/endings.
- **Heavy**: claims are empty or unsupported. Ask for source material, examples, data, or a sharper stance before rewriting.
