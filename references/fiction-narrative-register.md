# Fiction And Narrative Register

Use this reference for novels, short stories, narrative nonfiction, scene rewrites, fiction line editing, and narrative feedback such as 人物太平, 对白像作者代言, 观点性描写太多, 结尾说教, 像按规则写出来, or 去抽象后发干.

The goal is not to make prose uniformly short, concrete, cinematic, or colloquial. Preserve the story's point of view, narrative distance, genre promise, causal chain, character knowledge, and emotional temperature.

## Diagnose Upstream Before Editing Sentences

Check in this order:

1. **Scene engine**: who wants what, what resists them, what choice they make, and what state changes by the end.
2. **POV and information**: who can perceive, know, infer, remember, say, and understand each fact.
3. **Character agency**: whether events result from choices, defenses, mistakes, and costs instead of author placement.
4. **Dialogue action**: what each speaker is trying to obtain, avoid, hide, test, or force.
5. **World pressure**: how place, weather, money, work, institutions, bodies, crowds, and objects affect action.
6. **Narration and interiority**: what should be shown, what the viewpoint may think, and what must remain for the reader to infer.
7. **Rhythm and ending**: sentence/paragraph variation, emotional carry, chapter transitions, and where the narrative truly lands.

Do not promise a line edit can repair a missing scene turn, flat character engine, unfair revelation, or broken POV. At L1/L2, preserve plot and report the upstream problem. Enter L3/L4 only when authorized.

## Scene Gate

For each important scene, answer internally:

```text
Character wants A and uses B;
resistance C exposes or changes D;
the result is E rather than the expected F;
the next choice is now narrower, costlier, or different.
```

If the scene only delivers information, add no invented event at L2. Diagnose whether the information can instead be obtained through a cost, refusal, misunderstanding, relationship shift, or consequential choice.

AI-like narrative symptoms often originate upstream:

| Surface symptom | Likely cause | Correct move |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Characters keep summarizing the theme | Scene has no action carrying the meaning | Restore motive, choice, resistance, and consequence before polishing lines |
| Dialogue sounds interchangeable | Characters lack different wants, defenses, and knowledge | Rebuild verbal action and silence boundaries, not just vocabulary |
| Narration explains every emotion | Scene evidence is weak or the author distrusts the reader | Strengthen evidence, then retain only the interiority the viewpoint has earned |
| Setting feels like decoration | The world never changes what can happen | Make environment affect visibility, movement, risk, status, money, or timing |
| Ending announces the lesson | The scene has no final meaningful change | End on the last choice, reaction, changed relation, image, or unresolved pressure |
| Prose becomes dry after cleanup | Abstract/psychological language was deleted mechanically | Restore sparse, evidence-backed inner or relational echoes and vary cadence |

## POV And Knowledge Boundary

Lock the narrator, focal character, narrative distance, and time position before rewriting.

For every revelation or explanatory line, test four boundaries:

- **Knows**: could this person possess the fact?
- **Can say**: is it safe, permitted, or socially possible to say it here?
- **Wants to say**: does saying it help the person's immediate goal?
- **Can articulate**: would this person express it this clearly, completely, and conceptually?

Do not use a relative, witness, villager, colleague, or bystander as a free database. Fragment memory through body, bias, shame, self-protection, missing detail, mistaken order, objects, and refusal when the source supports it.

Avoid viewpoint leakage:

- facts noticed only because the author needs a clue
- conclusions that require another character's private knowledge
- polished social analysis unavailable to the current narrator
- sensory detail the focal character would not attend to under the current pressure

## Character Agency And Moral Friction

Do not protect a sympathetic narrator from meaningful participation. In satire, workplace, family, or social narratives, kindness may coexist with self-protection, silence, convenience, and minor complicity.

Test important characters for:

- immediate desire
- feared loss or shame
- habitual defense
- mistaken belief
- choice that affects someone else
- benefit gained from acting or staying silent
- consequence that later returns

Do not manufacture wrongdoing to make a character "complex." Use only choices supported by the source or authorized structural development.

## Dialogue As Verbal Action

For each key line, identify:

1. What the speaker wants from this listener now.
2. Why the speaker cannot or will not ask directly.
3. The chosen strategy: probe, flatter, deflect, shame, threaten, bargain, conceal, joke, plead, stall, or refuse.
4. How the listener resists, misreads, yields, or changes the subject.
5. What changes in information, relationship, risk, or available choices.

Delete or rebuild dialogue that only explains backstory, repeats known facts, states the theme, or sounds equally plausible in another character's mouth.

Differentiate voices through more than catchphrases:

- what the character notices first
- sentence length and completion
- concrete versus conceptual vocabulary
- status behavior and terms of address
- what they refuse to name
- how they lie, soften, interrupt, or remain silent

Do not make every exchange a clever duel. Ordinary people may be vague, repetitive, indirect, partially wrong, or unable to finish a thought.

## Scene Evidence And Earned Interiority

Prefer this sequence when it fits the narrative distance:

```text
perceived fact -> body/action/choice -> brief available thought -> reader inference
```

This is not a mandatory sentence formula. Keep direct thought when it is:

- available to the focal character now
- supported by something already perceived or experienced
- shaped by the character's vocabulary and blind spots
- shorter or less complete than the author's final interpretation
- needed for emotional continuity, not used to explain the theme

Rewrite when narration says what the reader should conclude before the scene has supplied evidence. Also rewrite when every feeling has been converted into a hand movement, object touch, weather detail, or silence. Mechanical externalization is another template.

## Description And World Pressure

Description must do at least one real job:

- alter movement, timing, visibility, or physical difficulty
- expose class, work, institution, custom, money, or power
- change what information is available
- affect intimacy, danger, embarrassment, or status
- give a recurring object or place a changed meaning through use

Do not add scenery merely to make prose "literary" or concrete. Do not turn a general texture checklist into a quota. Select details the viewpoint character would notice under the current desire and pressure.

Objects and repeated phrases work when their function changes. A title, form of address, seat, file name, utensil, door, or tool may first establish routine, later carry conflict, and finally return under a changed relationship. Do not explain the new meaning after the scene has made it legible.

## Information Release

For major information, check:

```text
trace -> plausible misreading -> cost -> partial confirmation -> later reinterpretation
```

Use this as a diagnostic pattern, not a required formula. A reveal fails when it is delivered free by one articulate witness, appears only at the ending, or depends on evidence the reader could never access. Preserve uncertainty until the story has paid for clarity.

## Endings

Distinguish the last event from the narrative endpoint. End where meaning, pressure, relation, or choice last changes.

Possible ending functions include:

- a decision that closes one option
- a reaction that revalues an earlier event
- a changed use of a repeated word or object
- an image that carries a consequence already earned
- a question or pressure that remains genuinely open
- a quiet return to routine whose meaning has changed

Remove logistics after that point unless they create a new consequence. Avoid:

- explicit moral summaries
- "he finally understood" epiphanies unsupported by action
- a polished aphorism after the real ending
- every chapter ending on a prop close-up, swallowed sentence, weather beat, or clipped one-line judgment

## Whole-Work Pass

For multi-chapter work, do not validate only sentence by sentence. Read in tracks:

1. **Adjacent chapters**: transitions, motive, emotional carry, repeated information.
2. **Character track**: desire, defense, mistake, choice, and cost across appearances.
3. **Information track**: who knows what, when, through which evidence, and at what cost.
4. **World/motif track**: whether settings, institutions, repeated words, and objects continue to exert pressure or change meaning.
5. **Ending track**: compare all chapter endings for repeated devices and identical emotional cadence.
6. **Temperature track**: check whether multiple local edits made the book uniformly dry, clipped, polished, solemn, or clever.

Automated keyword counts may locate passages; they cannot prove viewpoint, voice, emotional continuity, or narrative quality. Never use zero occurrences of abstract words, psychological verbs, or a phrase blacklist as a fiction acceptance criterion.

## Fiction Stop Gate

Before completion, verify:

- POV and narrative distance remain stable unless a shift is intentional.
- No character exceeds their knowledge or articulation boundary.
- Key scenes change a value, relationship, risk, understanding, or available choice.
- Important dialogue has an immediate action and consequence.
- Description belongs to the viewpoint and affects the scene.
- Interiority is neither author explanation nor mechanically purged.
- Repeated words/objects change function instead of merely repeating.
- The ending lands after the last meaningful change and does not explain its own effect.
- For long work, chapter transitions, character/information tracks, ending variety, and language temperature have been checked.
- Any unresolved L3/L4 problem is reported instead of hidden by surface polish.
